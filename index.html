01 / Profile

Engineer of the moment things fail

Jubayer Ahmed Sajid is a senior Aerospace Engineering student at Aviation and Aerospace University, Bangladesh (AAUB), specializing in impact dynamics, composite and lattice structures, and ANSYS-based advanced simulation. His work lives in the microseconds where a bird meets a fan blade, a soft body meets a stiffened panel, or a projectile meets Kevlar — and in the models that predict what happens next.

His undergraduate thesis develops machine-learning-based prediction of soft-body impacts on composite stiffened panels, pairing explicit dynamics with data-driven surrogates so that hours of solver time collapse into seconds of inference. He has published in MDPI Designs and Modelling, with further journal work accepted and under review at Technical Gazette and Wiley journals.

Beyond research, he has competed at the front of international design competitions — AIAA Design/Build/Fly and two Vertical Flight Society design competitions — and led the winning team at a national CAD championship. He is guided by Dr. Saiaf Bin Rayhan and Dr. Ivan Grgić, and is currently preparing PhD applications in computational mechanics and aerospace structures.
