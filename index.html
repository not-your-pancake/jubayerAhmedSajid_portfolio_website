— 01 / Profile

Engineer of the
moment things fail.

"Every structure has a worst millisecond. My work is to find it in simulation — before it happens in the sky."

Jubayer Ahmed Sajid is a senior Aerospace Engineering student at Aviation and Aerospace University, Bangladesh, specializing in impact dynamics, composite and lattice structures, and ANSYS-based advanced simulation. His work lives where a bird meets a fan blade, a projectile meets Kevlar, a soft body meets a stiffened panel — and in the models that predict what happens next.

His undergraduate thesis develops machine-learning-based prediction of soft-body impacts on composite stiffened panels, pairing explicit dynamics with data-driven surrogates so hours of solver time collapse into seconds of inference. He has published in MDPI Designs and Modelling, with further work accepted at Technical Gazette and under review at Wiley journals.

Beyond research, he has competed at the front of international design competitions — AIAA Design/Build/Fly and two Vertical Flight Society competitions — and led the winning team at a national CAD championship. Guided by Dr. Saiaf Bin Rayhan and Dr. Ivan Grgić, he is preparing PhD applications in computational mechanics and aerospace structures.
